## Findings of Fact

### 1. Relationship Between Parties

XX Venture Capital Investment Co., Ltd. (Plaintiff) and YY Technology Co., Ltd. (Defendant) signed a "Strategic Cooperation Agreement" in March 2021, agreeing that Plaintiff would invest RMB XXXX million in Defendant and hold 15% equity. ZZ Capital Management Co., Ltd. (Third Party) is the original shareholder of Defendant, holding 10% equity.

### 2. Timeline of Case Facts

**March 15, 2021**: Plaintiff and Defendant signed "Strategic Cooperation Agreement," agreeing that Plaintiff would provide RMB XXXX million investment for XX software platform R&D. Article 5 of the Agreement states: Within 90 days after the end of each fiscal year, Defendant shall distribute cash to shareholders at 30% of distributable profits for that year, and Plaintiff shall enjoy investment income according to its shareholding ratio.

**April 1, 2021**: Plaintiff transferred investment funds of RMB XXXX million to Defendant's designated account via bank transfer.

**May 10, 2021**: Plaintiff and Defendant signed a "Supplementary Agreement," agreeing to adjust the income distribution ratio in Article 5 of the original "Strategic Cooperation Agreement" to: Plaintiff may only obtain 20% of the excess portion as investment income when Defendant's annual net profit exceeds RMB XXXX million. This agreement was not passed through Defendant's shareholders' meeting resolution.

**June 20, 2021**: Defendant completed industrial and commercial registration changes, and Plaintiff formally held 15% equity in Defendant.

**January 10, 2022**: Defendant held a shareholders' meeting, resolving the 2021 profit distribution plan: distribute dividends to shareholders at 30% of net profit.

**March 5, 2022**: Defendant distributed dividends of RMB XX million to Plaintiff. Plaintiff believed that according to the "Supplementary Agreement," Defendant's 2021 net profit was RMB XXXX million, which did not exceed the RMB XXXX million threshold, so Plaintiff was not entitled to income distribution, and the RMB XX million was a friendly gesture.

**August 15, 2022**: Defendant sent a "Profit Distribution Explanation Letter" to Plaintiff, claiming that according to the "Supplementary Agreement," Plaintiff was not entitled to fixed-proportion income for 2021 and only received a dividend of RMB XX million.

**January 20, 2023**: Plaintiff filed a lawsuit with this Court, requesting confirmation of the invalidity of the "Supplementary Agreement" and demanding Defendant pay outstanding investment income and liquidated damages.

## Issues in Dispute

### Issue 1: Validity of the "Supplementary Agreement"

**Plaintiff's Claim**: The "Supplementary Agreement" is a true expression of both parties' intentions and is legally valid. As an investor, Plaintiff negotiated with Defendant to adjust the income distribution method, which falls within the scope of business judgment and does not violate legal provisions.

**Defendant's Claim**: The "Supplementary Agreement" was signed through mutual negotiation and agreement, representing a new arrangement of investment income between both parties, and is legally valid.

**Court's Determination**: According to Article 8 of Defendant's articles of association, major agreement changes between the Company and external investors must be passed through special shareholders' meeting resolution (approved by shareholders holding more than two-thirds of shares). The "Supplementary Agreement" was not passed through Defendant's shareholders' meeting resolution, violating Defendant's internal articles of association. Additionally, the "Supplementary Agreement" adjusted Plaintiff's income distribution conditions from a fixed ratio to excess decrement, substantially reducing Plaintiff's investment income and potentially harming Defendant and other shareholders' interests.

**Applicable Law**: Article 154 of the Civil Code of the People's Republic of China: A civil legal act where the actor and the counterparty maliciously collude to harm the legitimate rights and interests of others is invalid.

**Conclusion**: Confirmed that the "Supplementary Agreement" is invalid.
