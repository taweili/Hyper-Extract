# Equipment Failure Case Analysis Report

**Report Number**: FA-2024-001
**Preparation Date**: March 15, 2024
**Equipment Name**: Automated Assembly Line A1 - Main Motor
**Failure Date**: January 10, 2024

---

## 1. Failure Overview

### 1.1 Equipment Information

| Item | Content |
|------|--------|
| Equipment ID | MTR-001 |
| Equipment Name | Main Motor |
| Model | DM-750 |
| Manufacturer | Power Drive Co., Ltd. |
| Installation Location | Manufacturing Workshop A, Zone 1 |
| Commissioning Date | June 2022 |

### 1.2 Failure Phenomenon

At 9:30 AM on January 10, 2024, the main motor (MTR-001) of Automated Assembly Line A1 suddenly emitted abnormal sounds during operation, accompanied by significantly increased vibration. The operator immediately pressed the emergency stop button, and the equipment stopped running.

**Specific Phenomena**:
- Motor emitted sharp metal friction sound
- Vibration amplitude exceeded normal value by 3 times
- Bearing temperature rapidly rose to 85°C
- Inverter displayed abnormal current fluctuation

---

## 2. Failure Analysis

### 2.1 Initial Inspection

After arriving at the site, maintenance personnel conducted initial inspection:

1. **Visual Inspection**: No obvious damage to motor housing, no abnormal odor
2. **Insulation Test**: Winding insulation resistance normal, ruled out short circuit possibility
3. **Vibration Test**: Vibration value exceeded standard, mainly concentrated at bearing area
4. **Temperature Test**: Bearing seat temperature was high at 85°C

### 2.2 In-depth Analysis

After disassembly and inspection, the root cause of the failure was confirmed as follows:

**Main Cause**: Severe bearing wear

- Bearing raceway showed obvious wear marks
- Rolling element surface had spalling phenomenon
- Lubricating grease turned black and deteriorated, losing lubrication function

**Secondary Cause**: Long-term lack of preventive maintenance

- The motor had not undergone bearing replacement since commissioning
- Lubricating grease was not regularly supplemented as required
- Operating load had been at high-load state for a long time

---

## 3. Handling Measures

### 3.1 Emergency Handling

1. Immediately stopped equipment operation and cut off power
2. Marked fault status and hung warning signs
3. Started backup motor to maintain production

### 3.2 Root Cause Treatment

1. **Bearing Replacement**: Replaced bearing with same specification (Model: 6208-2RS)
2. **Lubricating Grease Supplementation**: Used high-quality lubricating grease (Model: L-XBCHA3)
3. **Bearing Seat Cleaning**: Thoroughly removed old lubricating grease and impurities
4. **Electrical Testing**: Tested winding resistance to ensure normal electrical performance
