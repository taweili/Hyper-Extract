# Automated Assembly Line A1 Safety Management and Emergency Response Manual

**Document Number**: SMC-2024-001
**Version**: v2.0
**Preparation Date**: February 2024
**Scope**: All operators of Automated Assembly Line A1

---

## Part 1: Safety Control

### Chapter 1: General Provisions

#### 1.1 Purpose

This manual is specially formulated to strengthen safety production management of Automated Assembly Line A1, prevent and reduce production safety accidents, and protect employees' lives, safety and physical health.

#### 1.2 Scope of Application

This manual applies to all operational activities of Automated Assembly Line A1, including equipment operation, maintenance, inspection, etc.

### Chapter 2: Hazard Identification

#### 2.1 Equipment Hazards

| No. | Hazard Name | Hazard Factor | Possible Consequences |
|-----|-------------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | CNC Machining Center | High temperature components, rotating parts | Burns, mechanical injury |
| 2 | Industrial Robot Arm | Moving parts, clamping force | Crushing injury, impact injury |
| 3 | Cooling System | High-pressure liquid, temperature changes | Burns, frostbite |
| 4 | Hydraulic Station | High-pressure oil, seal aging | High-pressure jet injury |
| 5 | Electrical Control Cabinet | Electrical components, high-voltage power | Electric shock, electrocution |

### Chapter 3: Risk Points and Control Measures

#### 3.1 Mechanical Equipment Risks

| Risk Point | Risk Level | Control Measures | Responsible Person |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| CNC machining center operation | Major risk | 1. Strictly follow operating procedures 2. Wear safety glasses and protective gloves 3. Prohibit opening protective doors while equipment is running 4. Regularly check effectiveness of protective devices | Equipment supervisor |
| Robot arm operation area | Major risk | 1. Set safety fences and warning signs 2. Install light curtain protection devices 3. Confirm no personnel in safety area before operation 4. Strictly prohibit unauthorized entry into operation area | Shift leader |
| Hydraulic system maintenance | High risk | 1. Release pressure before maintenance 2. Wear protective gloves and safety glasses 3. Use professional tools for maintenance 4. Conduct pressure test after maintenance | Maintenance worker |

#### 3.2 Electrical Safety Risks

| Risk Point | Risk Level | Control Measures | Responsible Person |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Electrical cabinet operation | Major risk | 1. Only qualified electrical workers may operate 2. Perform voltage check before operation 3. Wear insulated shoes and gloves 4. Prohibit live work | Electrician |
| Temporary electricity use | High risk | 1. Complete temporary electricity approval 2. Use qualified electrical equipment 3. Regularly check line insulation performance 4. Implement one-machine-one-switch-one-protection | Equipment supervisor |
| Static electricity protection | General risk | 1. Wear anti-static work clothes 2. Ensure reliable equipment grounding 3. Maintain appropriate ambient humidity | Safety officer |

#### 3.3 Chemical Safety Risks

| Risk Point | Risk Level | Control Measures | Responsible Person |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Coolant use | High risk | 1. Wear protective gloves and safety glasses 2. Keep workplace ventilated 3. Avoid direct skin contact 4. Regularly check coolant concentration | Equipment supervisor |
| Lubricating grease | General risk | 1. Wear protective gloves 2. Avoid contact with mouth and eyes 3. Use special tools for adding 4. Unified recycling of waste oil | Maintenance worker |
| Cleaning agent use | High risk | 1. Use in ventilated areas 2. Wear gas masks 3. Store away from fire sources 4. Operate according to MSDS requirements | Shift leader |
