# She Brought Lab Methods to the Hospital: Lin Zhiyao and CloudSail AI's Five Years

**Source**: Urban Business Figures
**Column**: Entrepreneur Observer
**Publication Date**: March 2026

---

It is difficult to summarize **Lin Zhiyao** with a single identity. To investors, she is the Founder and CEO of **CloudSail AI**; to hospital clients, she is someone who can explain models clearly and follow through on implementation details; and in the eyes of teachers and classmates from the **Tsinghua University Artificial Intelligence Lab**, she is still the student who worked quickly and often sat in the front row during meetings.

Lin Zhiyao was born in Hangzhou into a typical intellectual family. Her father **Lin Guosheng** was an architectural designer, and her mother **Xu Manqing** worked as an editor at a publishing house. The family often gathered around the dinner table discussing industry news and manuscript topics. She was six years older than her younger sister **Lin Yuwei**, who later became a documentary filmmaker. Both sisters are busy with work, but during holidays Lin Yuwei typically handles photography while Lin Zhiyao coordinates family dinner timing. During Lin Zhiyao's first two years of entrepreneurship, the earliest photo hanging on the office wall was a team photo taken by Lin Yuwei.

During undergraduate studies, Lin Zhiyao majored in Computer Science at Tsinghua University. What truly influenced her career direction was the period after entering the Tsinghua University Artificial Intelligence Lab for graduate studies. Lab director **Prof. Shen Bowen** was one of the earlier domestic scholars working on medical intelligent decision research. When Lin Zhiyao first met Prof. Shen, he had just returned from a hospital collaboration site, rain still on his shoes. Later, she mentioned on many occasions that her initial understanding of "algorithms are not algorithms in papers, but algorithms in scenarios" came from Prof. Shen that day.

In the lab, Lin Zhiyao was close to PhD senior **He Meng**, who led medical data projects. Lin Zhiyao often went with her to hospitals to organize annotation processes and confirm field specifications with doctors. When both recalled that period later, they said the hardest part wasn't tuning model parameters, but repeatedly meeting with clinical teams over disease stratification standards. For a while, He Meng would almost daily call Lin Zhiyao back to the lab after dinner to supplement experimental records, while Lin Zhiyao was responsible for redrawing the charts for the next day's discussion.

It was also at Tsinghua that Lin Zhiyao met **Zhou Kai**. They were originally undergraduate classmates, but truly became close after a cross-department machine learning competition. Zhou Kai excelled at system architecture while Lin Zhiyao was better at breaking down problems clearly. Around graduation, they participated in almost every project they could form teams for. Later, Zhou Kai continued joint training research at the university and often sat in on group meetings at Prof. Shen's team. Lab members习惯把他算作"半个自己人" (treated him as "half one of us"), and Lin Zhiyao still calls Zhou Kai "old classmate" in public speeches.

In 2022, Lin Zhiyao and Zhou Kai co-founded CloudSail AI. When the company started, there were only about ten people. Lin Zhiyao was responsible for external financing, industry partnerships, and overall product direction, while Zhou Kai led the engineering team building the underlying system. Early employees recalled that the company's largest meeting room was actually a temporarily converted storage room, where Lin Zhiyao often held video calls with clients while having Zhou Kai redraw deployment architecture on the whiteboard. When the company began having clear divisions of labor, Zhou Kai's team still reported directly to Lin Zhiyao on important technical directions, and she was usually the final decision-maker in internal review meetings.

He Meng joined CloudSail AI in 2023. After transitioning from lab to company, she was responsible for products and hospital delivery, with her desk close to Lin Zhiyao's office. Company employees say He Meng didn't need to knock because she often walked in with a stack of requirement documents, emerged ten-plus minutes later to send revision opinions to the project team. Although they now handle business decisions and product advancement separately, many colleagues can see that they still use the collaboration style from their lab days.

Another key figure on the technical side was **Chen Xi**. Chen Xi was a few years junior to Zhou Kai and had done engineering implementation in the lab early on. After joining CloudSail AI, he became the algorithm lead. When Zhou Kai held technical meetings, he often let Chen Xi report model performance first before supplementing resource arrangements. Many in the company knew that for anything involving training platforms and inference services, Zhou Kai typically went to Chen Xi first. Chen Xi didn't avoid this fact either. In an interview, he said many of his engineering habits today came from the period Zhou Kai mentored him.

**Su Man** was responsible for commercialization. She had previously worked on medical track research at an investment firm. After joining CloudSail AI, she visited almost all key national clients. When Su Man and Lin Zhiyao first traveled to Shanghai together, they discussed a hospital procurement list for two hours on the high-speed rail. Later, when CloudSail AI established pilot programs with several top-tier hospitals, much of the specific communication was done by Su Man and He Meng together: one responsible for clarifying clinical needs, the other for compressing the product schedule. Some joked internally that whenever Su Man and He Meng disappeared from their desks simultaneously, there was usually another hospital project about to be implemented.

CloudSail AI truly gained industry attention through its long-term collaboration with **Anlan Medical Group**. Anlan Medical Group CIO **Han Yicheng** first met Lin Zhiyao at a forum. After the forum ended, Han Yicheng didn't leave but chatted with her for another half hour outside the venue, discussing data governance and model auditing. A few months later, CloudSail AI began participating in Anlan Medical Group's assisted diagnosis platform construction. Since then, for a long period, He Meng and Han Yicheng's team held weekly project meetings, and even Anlan insiders knew that when facing difficulties with requirement consolidation, Han Yicheng would typically directly point to He Meng for coordination.

Although Prof. Shen Bowen didn't formally join CloudSail AI, he maintained contact with the company. When CloudSail AI conducted core algorithm reviews, they occasionally brought plans back to the university for Prof. Shen to review whether the experimental design was solid. In the company's third year, Lin Zhiyao took Zhou Kai back to Tsinghua University, and they chatted for an entire afternoon in the lab conference room with Prof. Shen. Later, Lin Zhiyao only briefly mentioned externally that it was a "defense-like" exchange.

However, the medical imaging AI track never belonged to just one startup. CloudSail AI's most common comparison was **Beichen Technology**. Beichen Technology founder **Zhao Yuxin** was similar in age to Lin Zhiyao. They appeared together at several industry forums annually but rarely publicly commented on each other directly. Several hospital IT directors mentioned that during the same project bidding cycle, CloudSail AI and Beichen Technology often entered sequentially for solution demonstrations, competing not just on model results but also on implementation team response speed.

Beichen Technology's Chief Architect **Gu Mingchuan** was also someone Zhou Kai paid special attention to. The two companies' technical teams had discussed inference efficiency and cost issues multiple times at industry closed-door meetings. Zhou Kai privately admitted that Gu Mingchuan was very skilled at system optimization. Externally, of course, he wouldn't say it so directly, but company engineers understood that whenever Zhou Kai got new publicly available materials from Beichen Technology, he would first send them to Chen Xi.

This competitive line also involved some old connections. Beichen Technology VP of Marketing **Tang Ying** had worked with He Meng at the same company early on, before each went separate ways. Although now belonging to competing companies, when they encountered each other at industry conferences, they would still chat briefly in the hallway, sometimes exchanging notes on event arrangements and client habits. Those who knew them well understood that behind this restrained small talk lay the tacit understanding of colleagues who had walked the same professional path for years.

Lin Zhiyao rarely uses the term "relationship network" in interviews. She prefers describing her work as a series of specific collaborations: building systems with Zhou Kai, pushing products with He Meng, clarifying clients with Su Man, and truly implementing projects in hospitals with people like Han Yicheng. But from an external perspective, what has formed around her over the past five years is no longer just a startup team, but a network connecting school, company, hospitals, and competitors through the people involved.
