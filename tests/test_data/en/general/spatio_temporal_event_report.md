# 2024 Southern China Heavy Rain and Flood Disaster Rescue Operation Report

## Event Overview

From June 15 to 25, 2024, southern China experienced rare prolonged heavy rainfall, causing severe rainstorm and flood disasters in the middle and lower reaches of the Yangtze River and the Pearl River basin. This disaster had a wide impact, long duration, and strong destructive power, causing significant casualties and property losses.

## Disaster Overview

### Time Span

June 15 to July 10, 2024

### Affected Regions

- **Province A**: Cities B, C, D
- **Province E**: Cities F, G, H
- **Province I**: Cities J, K, L
- **Province M**: Cities N, O, P
- **Province Q**: Cities R, S, T

### Meteorological Data

On June 15, the Central Meteorological Observatory issued a red warning for heavy rain. This was the first river basin-level heavy rain red warning issued in nearly a decade.

## Rescue Operation Timeline

### Phase 1: Early Warning Response (June 15-17)

**June 15, 10:00 AM** - Beijing
The National Flood Control and Drought Relief Headquarters activated Level 2 emergency response, requiring all regions to take preventive measures.

**June 15, 2:00 PM** - City F
The Provincial E Flood Control and Drought Relief Command held an emergency meeting to deploy provincial flood control work. City F's Flood Control Command activated Level 3 emergency response.

**June 16, 8:00 AM** - All Affected Provinces
Provinces A, E, I, M, and Q all activated Level 1 emergency response. Armed police forces and militia reserve forces entered standby status.

**June 17, 6:00 AM** - City C
A scenic area in Province A's City C was urgently closed. Water levels at a section of the Xiang River reached the warning level of 36 meters.

### Phase 2: Emergency Rescue (June 18-22)

**June 18, 3:00 AM** - City D
A levee breach occurred in a district of City D. A unit from the PLA Central Theater urgently deployed 2,000 officers and soldiers to transfer 15,000 trapped residents overnight.

**June 18, 10:30 AM** - City J
Severe waterlogging occurred in a district of Province I's City J. Armed Police Provincial I Corps deployed 500 officers and soldiers with 30 assault boats for rescue operations.

**June 19, 8:00 AM** - City F
A levee seepage incident occurred in a district of City F. Two hundred personnel from the Army Engineering University's professional flood control and rescue force urgently rushed to the scene and successfully handled the emergency.

**June 20, 2:00 AM** - City O
During the transfer of trapped residents in a county of Province M's City O, an assault boat capsized, causing 3 residents to fall into the water. Armed Police Provincial M Corps officers and soldiers immediately jumped into the water to rescue them. All were rescued.

**June 21, 3:00 PM** - City S
A landslide occurred in a district of Province Q's City S, causing residential building collapse. Armed Police Provincial Q Corps deployed 100 officers and soldiers for search and rescue of buried residents.

**June 22, 9:00 AM** - City K
A town in Province I's City K became isolated, with over 2,000 residents trapped. An Air Force unit deployed 6 helicopter sorties to airdrop 5 tons of rescue supplies.

### Phase 3: Post-Disaster Resettlement (June 23 - July 5)

**June 23, 8:00 AM** - All Affected Areas
The Ministry of Emergency Management activated Level 4 disaster relief emergency response and arranged RMB 1 billion in central disaster relief funds.

**June 24, 10:00 AM** - City B
Province A established a temporary settlement at an exhibition center, accommodating 3,000 disaster-affected residents. Social donations of supplies arrived successively.

**June 26, 2:00 PM** - City F
Province E established a temporary settlement at a sports center, accommodating 4,500 disaster-affected residents.

**June 28, 8:00 AM** - City J
Province I established a temporary settlement at a gymnasium, accommodating 2,800 disaster-affected residents.

**July 1, 9:00 AM** - All Affected Areas
The National Health Commission organized medical and epidemic prevention teams to carry out medical treatment and sanitation and epidemic prevention work in disaster areas.

**July 3, 10:00 AM** - City D
Province A carried out post-disaster reconstruction planning for the most severely affected district in City D and initiated housing reconstruction work.

### Phase 4: Recovery and Reconstruction (July 6-10)

**July 6, 8:00 AM** - All Affected Provinces
The National Development and Reform Commission issued RMB 1.5 billion in central budget investment for post-disaster recovery and reconstruction.

**July 8, 9:00 AM** - City F
Province E held a post-disaster reconstruction work conference and identified 58 key restoration projects.

**July 10, 2:00 PM** - Beijing
The State Council held an executive meeting to deploy post-disaster recovery and reconstruction work, requiring efforts to restore normal production and living order within one year.
