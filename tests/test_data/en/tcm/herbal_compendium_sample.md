# Materia Medica Classics Sample

## Ginseng (Ren Shen)

### Name Explanation
Ginseng, also known as Huang Shen, Xue Shen, Ren Xian, Gui Gai, Shen Cao, Tu Jing, Di Jing, Hai Yu, Zhou Mian Huan Dan.

### Collected Explanations
"Bie Lu" states: Ginseng grows in the valleys of Shangdang and Liaodong. The roots are harvested in February, April, and early August, scraped with bamboo knife and dried in shade, must not be exposed to wind.

### Properties
Sweet, slightly cold, non-toxic.

### Main Indications
Tonifies the five zang organs, calms the spirit and consciousness, stabilizes the soul and ethereal soul, stops palpitations and fright, eliminates pathogenic factors, improves vision, opens the heart and benefits intelligence. Long-term use lightens the body and extends lifespan.

### Attached Formulas
1. Syndrome of yin collapse and yang exhaustion: Ginseng one liang, aconite one piece (processed), divided into four portions, decocted with water for oral administration.
2. Weak spleen and stomach: Ginseng, white atractylodes each two qian, poria, licorica each one qian, decocted with water for oral administration.

## Licorice (Gan Cao)

### Name Explanation
Licorice, also known as Mi Gan, Mi Cao, Mei Cao, Cao, Ling Tong, Guo Lao.

### Collected Explanations
"Bie Lu" states: Licorice grows in the valleys of western Henan and Jishan, and upper commandery. Roots are harvested on the second and eighth month on auspicious days, dried in sun, ready in ten days.

### Properties
Sweet, balanced, non-toxic.

### Main Indications
Cold and heat pathogens in the five zang and six fu organs, strengthens tendons and bones, promotes muscle growth, doubles qi and strength, treats金山 (unspecified) swelling, relieves toxicity. Long-term use lightens the body and extends lifespan.

### Attached Formulas
1. Sore throat in cold damage: Licorice two liang, platycodon one liang, decocted with water for oral administration.
2. Pediatric heat cough: Licorice two liang, pig gall juice soaked for five nights, roasted and ground into powder, made into honey pills the size of mung beans, ten pills taken after meals with thin mint soup.

## Astragalus (Huang Qi)

### Name Explanation
Astragalus, also known as Dai Shen, Dai Shen, Bai Ben, Wang Sun, Bai Yao Mian, Mian Huang Qi, Du Shen, Shu Zhi, Huang Qi.

### Collected Explanations
"Bie Lu" states: Astragalus grows in the valleys of Shu commandery, Baishui, and Hanzhong. Roots are harvested in February and October, dried in shade.

### Properties
Sweet, slightly warm, non-toxic.

### Main Indications
Long-term ulcers and decayed sores, expels pus and relieves pain, treats severe wind and leprosy, five hemorrhoids and mouse fistula, tonifies deficiency, pediatric hundred diseases.

### Attached Formulas
1. Qi deficiency with night sweats: Astragalus two liang, white atractylodes one liang, fangfeng half liang, ground into powder, three qian each time, decocted with water for oral administration.
2. Qi deficiency with fatigue: Astragalus, ginseng each one liang, decocted with water for oral administration.
