# Famous Physician Case Records Sample

## Taiyang Wind-Strike Case

### Symptoms
Fever, sweating, aversion to wind, moderate pulse, nasal wheezing, dry retching.

### Pattern Differentiation
Taiyang wind-strike: the yang is floating and the yin is weak. Yang floating generates heat; yin weakness causes spontaneous sweating. Gradual aversion to cold, slight aversion to wind, mild fever, nasal wheezing and dry retching indicate that Guizhi Decoction should be the primary treatment.

### Treatment Method
Releases the muscles and expels pathogens, harmonizes the Ying and Wei.

### Formula
Guizhi Decoction (Cinnamon Twig Decoction): Cinnamon twig three liang (removed bark), peony three liang, licorica two liang (honey-fried), fresh ginger three liang (sliced), jujube twelve pieces (broken).

### Preparation and Administration
Grind five ingredients, decoct with seven sheng of water over gentle heat until three sheng remains. Remove the residue, let cool to appropriate temperature, take one sheng. After taking, drink one sheng plus of hot thin porridge to assist the medicinal force. Cover with clothing for approximately one hour until the whole body has slight sweating is best. Do not cause heavy sweating like water flowing, or the disease will not be eliminated.

### Case Analysis
This case is a typical Taiyang wind-strike pattern. The patient was affected by wind pathogen, defensive qi is insecure, and ying yin is leaking outward, hence symptoms of fever, sweating, and aversion to wind. Treatment with Guizhi Decoction releases the muscles and expels pathogens, harmonizes ying and wei, removing the pathogen and restoring proper qi.

## Taiyang Cold-Damage Case

### Symptoms
Headache, fever, body pain, lower back pain, joint pain, aversion to wind, no sweating with wheezing.

### Pattern Differentiation
In Taiyang disease with headache, fever, body pain, lower back pain, joint pain, aversion to wind, no sweating with wheezing, Mahuang Decoction should be the primary treatment.

### Treatment Method
Promotes sweating and releases the exterior, diffuses the lung to relieve asthma.

### Formula
Mahuang Decoction (Ephedra Decoction): Ephedra three liang (removed nodes), cinnamon twig two liang (removed bark), licorica one liang (honey-fried), apricot kernel seventy pieces (removed skin and tip).

### Preparation and Administration
Decoct four ingredients with nine sheng of water. First decoct mahuang, reduce by two sheng, remove the foam, add the other herbs, decoct until two and a half sheng remains. Remove the residue, take eight ge warm. Cover to induce slight sweating. No need for hot porridge. Follow the same guidelines as Guizhi Decoction.

### Case Analysis
This case is a typical Taiyang cold-damage pattern. The patient was affected by cold pathogen, defensive yang is restrained, and ying yin is stagnated, hence symptoms of headache, fever, body pain, and no sweating. Treatment with Mahuang Decoction promotes sweating and releases the exterior, diffuses the lung to relieve asthma, causing the cold pathogen to resolve through sweating.

## Shaoyang Disease Case

### Symptoms
Alternating chills and fever, chest and hypochondriac fullness, dispirited and reluctant to eat, irritability, frequent vomiting, bitter taste, dry throat, dizziness.

### Pattern Differentiation
When wind-cold damage occurs for five to six days with alternating chills and fever, chest and hypochondriac fullness, dispirited and reluctant to eat, irritability and frequent vomiting, or chest discomfort without vomiting, or thirst, or abdominal pain, or hypochondriac hardness, or palpitations below the heart and urinary difficulty, or no thirst with slight fever externally, or cough—Xiaochaihu Decoction should be the primary treatment.

### Treatment Method
Harmonizes Shaoyang.

### Formula
Xiaochaihu Decoction (Minor Bupleurum Decoction): Bupleurum half jin, scutellaria three liang, ginseng three liang, licorica three liang (honey-fried), fresh ginger three liang (sliced), jujube twelve pieces (broken), pinellia half sheng (washed).

### Preparation and Administration
Decoct seven ingredients with one dou and two sheng of water until six sheng remains. Remove the residue, decoct again until three sheng remains. Take one sheng warm, three times daily.

### Case Analysis
This case is a typical Shaoyang disease pattern. The patient was affected by pathogen invading Shaoyang, the pivot mechanism is unbalanced, hence symptoms of alternating chills and fever, chest and hypochondriac fullness, irritability, and frequent vomiting. Treatment with Xiaochaihu Decoction harmonizes Shaoyang, removing the pathogen and curing the disease.
