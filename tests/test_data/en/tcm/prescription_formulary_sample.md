# Classical Formula Sample

## Guizhi Tang (Cinnamon Twig Decoction)

### Composition
Cinnamon twig three liang (removed bark), peony three liang, licorica two liang (honey-fried), fresh ginger three liang (sliced), jujube twelve pieces (broken).

### Preparation and Administration
Grind five ingredients, decoct with seven sheng of water over gentle heat until three sheng remains. Remove the residue, let cool to appropriate temperature, take one sheng. After taking, drink one sheng plus of hot thin porridge to assist the medicinal force. Cover with clothing for approximately one hour until the whole body has slight sweating is best. Do not cause heavy sweating like water flowing, or the disease will not be eliminated. If sweating occurs and the disease is cured, stop taking the remaining portion. If sweating does not occur, take again following the same method. If still no sweating, take again shortening the interval, approximately three portions within half a day. For severe disease, take continuously day and night, observe throughout; if after taking one portion the disease remains, take another portion; if sweating does not occur, may take up to two or three portions.

### Functions and Indications
Releases the muscles and expels pathogens, harmonizes the Ying and Wei. Main treatment of Taiyang wind-strike: yang is floating and yin is weak, yang floating generates heat, yin weakness causes spontaneous sweating. Patient presents with slight aversion to cold, slight aversion to wind, mild fever, nasal wheezing and dry retching.

### Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi (Monarch-Minister-Assistant-Emissary) Analysis
- **Monarch (Jun)**: Cinnamon twig, acrid and warm, releases the muscles and expels pathogens, warms and unblocks the channels.
- **Minister (Chen)**: Peony, sour and bitter, slightly cold, nourishes yin and constricts ying.
- **Assistant (Zuo)**: Fresh ginger, acrid and warm, assists cinnamon twig in releasing the exterior; jujube, sweet and warm, tonifies qi and supplements the middle.
- **Emissary (Shi)**: Licorica, sweet and warm, harmonizes all the medicinals.

## Mahuang Tang (Ephedra Decoction)

### Composition
Ephedra three liang (removed nodes), cinnamon twig two liang (removed bark), licorica one liang (honey-fried), apricot kernel seventy pieces (removed skin and tip).

### Preparation and Administration
Decoct four ingredients with nine sheng of water. First decoct mahuang, reduce by two sheng, remove the foam, add the other herbs, decoct until two and a half sheng remains. Remove the residue, take eight ge warm. Cover to induce slight sweating. No need for hot porridge. Follow the same guidelines as Guizhi Decoction.

### Functions and Indications
Promotes sweating and releases the exterior, diffuses the lung to relieve asthma. Main treatment of Taiyang disease: headache, fever, body pain, lower back pain, joint pain, aversion to wind, no sweating with wheezing.

### Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi Analysis
- **Monarch (Jun)**: Ephedra, acrid and warm, promotes sweating and releases the exterior, diffuses the lung to relieve asthma.
- **Minister (Chen)**: Cinnamon twig, acrid and warm, releases the muscles and expels pathogens, warms and unblocks the channels.
- **Assistant (Zuo)**: Apricot kernel, bitter and warm, descends and directs lung qi downward, relieves asthma and stops coughing.
- **Emissary (Shi)**: Licorica, sweet and warm, harmonizes all the medicinals.

## Xiaochaihu Tang (Minor Bupleurum Decoction)

### Composition
Bupleurum half jin, scutellaria three liang, ginseng three liang, licorica three liang (honey-fried), fresh ginger three liang (sliced), jujube twelve pieces (broken), pinellia half sheng (washed).

### Preparation and Administration
Decoct seven ingredients with one dou and two sheng of water until six sheng remains. Remove the residue, decoct again until three sheng remains. Take one sheng warm, three times daily.

### Functions and Indications
Harmonizes Shaoyang. Main treatment: when wind-cold damage occurs for five to six days with alternating chills and fever, chest and hypochondriac fullness, dispirited and reluctant to eat, irritability and frequent vomiting; or chest discomfort without vomiting; or thirst; or abdominal pain; or hypochondriac hardness; or palpitations below the heart and urinary difficulty; or no thirst with slight fever externally; or cough.

### Jun-Chen-Zuo-Shi Analysis
- **Monarch (Jun)**: Bupleurum, bitter and neutral, harmonizes Shaoyang, courses the liver and gallbladder.
- **Minister (Chen)**: Scutellaria, bitter and cold, clears and drains Shaoyang heat.
- **Assistant (Zuo)**: Ginseng and jujube, tonify qi and fortify the spleen; pinellia and fresh ginger, harmonize the stomach and direct rebellion downward.
- **Emissary (Shi)**: Licorica, harmonizes all the medicinals.
