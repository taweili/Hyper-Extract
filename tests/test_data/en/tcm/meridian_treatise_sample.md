# Meridian and Acupoint Treatise Sample

## Twelve Primary Channels and Their Pathways

### Hand Taiyin Lung Channel

Begins in the middle jiao, descends to connect with the large intestine, returns to loop around the stomach opening, passes through the diaphragm, and belongs to the lung. From the lung system, it emerges laterally at the axilla, descends along the inner aspect of the upper arm, travels anterior to the hand Shaoyin and hand Jueyin channels, reaches the inner aspect of the elbow, ascends along the radial border of the upper aspect of the forearm, enters the cun Kou (radial pulse), ascends to the thenar eminence, follows the thenar border, and emerges at the tip of the thumb. Its branch: from the area posterior to the wrist, emerges directly at the medial border of the index finger, and ends at its tip.

### Hand Yangming Large Intestine Channel

Begins at the tip of the index finger, follows the superior border of the index finger, emerges between the two metacarpal bones at Hegu, ascends between the two tendons on the radial side of the wrist, travels along the superior border of the forearm, enters the lateral aspect of the elbow, ascends along the lateral anterior aspect of the upper arm, reaches the shoulder, emerges at the anterior border of the shoulder socket, ascends to exit at the meeting point of the spine and shoulder, descends to enter the supraclavicular fossa, connects with the lung, passes through the diaphragm, belongs to the large intestine. Its branch: from the supraclavicular fossa, ascends the neck, penetrates the cheek, enters the lower teeth, returns and circles the lips, crosses at Renzhong, with the left going to the right and right to the left, ascends beside the nose to the lateral side of the nose.

### Foot Yangming Stomach Channel

Begins at the side of the nose, meets the foot Taiyang channel at Yingtang, descends along the lateral aspect of the nose, enters the upper teeth, returns and circles the lips, descends to Chengjiang, returns along the inferior border of the jaw, emerges at Dagying, follows Jiache, ascends in front of the ear, passes through Kukezhuren, follows the hairline to the forehead. Its branch: from Dagying anteriorly, descends to Renying, follows the throat, enters the supraclavicular fossa, descends through the diaphragm, belongs to the stomach, connects with the spleen. Its direct branch: from the supraclavicular fossa, descends along the inner aspect of the breast, descends beside the umbilicus, enters Qijie, the Stomach Qi Street. Its branch: originates from the stomach opening, descends along the interior of the abdomen, reaches Qijie where it joins the main channel. From there it descends to Biguan, reaches Futu, descends through the knee cup, follows the lateral border of the tibia, descends to the dorsum of the foot, enters the middle toe. Its branch: descends three cun below the knee, and enters the lateral side of the middle toe. Its branch: separates at the dorsum of the foot, enters the great toe, emerges at its tip.

### Foot Taiyin Spleen Channel

Begins at the tip of the great toe, follows the medial border of the great toe, passes posterior to the medial malleolus, ascends along the anterior border of the medial malleolus, ascends along the posterior aspect of the tibia, crosses anterior to the foot Shaoyin channel, ascends along the anterior border of the thigh, enters the abdomen, belongs to the spleen, connects with the stomach, passes through the diaphragm, attaches to the throat, connects with the tongue root, spreads under the tongue. Its branch: again separates from the stomach, ascends through the diaphragm, and enters the heart.

### Hand Shaoyin Heart Channel

Begins in the heart, exits to belong to the heart connecting system, descends through the diaphragm, connects with the small intestine. Its branch: from the heart connecting system, ascends alongside the throat, connects with the eye connecting system. Its direct branch: returns from the heart connecting system, ascends to the lung, descends to emerge at the axilla, descends along the posterior border of the inner aspect of the upper arm, travels posterior to the hand Taiyin and hand Jueyin channels, reaches the inner aspect of the elbow, follows the posterior border of the inner aspect of the forearm, reaches the tip of the styloid process of the ulna, enters the palm on the posterior border of the ulnar side, follows the inner side of the little finger, and emerges at its tip.

### Hand Taiyang Small Intestine Channel

Begins at the tip of the little finger, follows the lateral border of the hand, ascends at the wrist, emerges between the ulna and radius, ascends along the inferior border of the radius, emerges between the two tendons on the lateral side of the elbow, ascends along the posterior border of the lateral aspect of the upper arm, exits at the shoulder joint, circles the scapula, crosses at the shoulder to meet the channel, enters the supraclavicular fossa, connects with the heart, follows the throat through the diaphragm, reaches the stomach, belongs to the small intestine. Its branch: from the supraclavicular fossa, follows the neck, ascends the cheek, reaches the lateral canthus, returns and enters the ear. Its branch: separates at the cheek, ascends to the nose, reaches the medial canthus, diagonally connects to the zygomatic region.

## Commonly Used Acupoints

### Hegu (LI4)

Location: On the dorsum of the hand, between the 1st and 2nd metacarpal bones, at the midpoint of the 2nd metacarpal bone's radial side.

Indications: Headache, red swollen painful eyes, nosebleed, toothache, lockjaw, facial paralysis, deafness, mumps, sore throat, fever without sweating, excessive sweating, abdominal pain, constipation, amenorrhea, difficult labor.

### Zusanli (ST36)

Location: On the anterolateral aspect of the lower leg, 3 cun below the Dubi, one finger-breadth from the anterior border of the tibia.

Indications: Stomach pain, vomiting, dysphagia, abdominal distension, diarrhea, dysentery, constipation, breast abscess, intestinal abscess, lower limb pain and numbness, edema, manic-depressive disorder, beriberi, emaciation due to deficiency.

### Sanyinjiao (SP6)

Location: On the medial aspect of the lower leg, 3 cun above the tip of the medial malleolus, posterior to the medial border of the tibia.

Indications: Intestinal rumbling, abdominal distension, diarrhea, menstrual disorders, leukorrhea, vaginal prolapse, infertility, difficult labor, seminal emission, impotence, enuresis, hernia, insomnia, lower limb flaccidity and numbness, beriberi.

### Guanyuan (CV4)

Location: On the lower abdomen, on the anterior midline, 3 cun below the center of the umbilicus.

Indications: Stroke collapse syndrome, deficiency exhaustion, emaciation and weakness, lower abdominal pain, cholera vomiting and diarrhea, dysentery, rectal prolapse, hernia, bloody stools, bloody urine, dysuria, frequent urination, urinary retention, seminal emission, turbid white leukorrhea, impotence, premature ejaculation, menstrual disorders, amenorrhea, dysmenorrhea, vaginal prolapse, flooding and spotting, vulvar itching, lochia, retained placenta, wasting thirst, dizziness.
