## Chapter 1: Respiratory Diseases

### 1.1 Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

#### 1.1.1 Etiology and Pathogenesis
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a preventable and treatable disease characterized by persistent airflow limitation that is usually progressive and associated with an enhanced chronic inflammatory response in the airways and lung to noxious particles or gases.

**Risk Factors**:
- **Environmental factors**: Tobacco smoke, occupational dust and chemicals, air pollution, infection
- **Individual factors**: Genetic factors (such as alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency), abnormal lung development, airway hyperresponsiveness

**Pathogenesis**:
1. Airway inflammation: Neutrophils, macrophages, T lymphocytes and other inflammatory cells participate
2. Oxidative stress: Increased oxidant production, decreased antioxidant capacity
3. Protease-antiprotease imbalance: Increased protease activity, decreased antiprotease activity
4. Airway remodeling: Structural changes in airways leading to airflow limitation

### 1.2 Bronchial Asthma

#### 1.2.1 Definition and Classification
Bronchial asthma is a heterogeneous disease characterized by chronic airway inflammation involving multiple cells (such as eosinophils, mast cells, T lymphocytes, neutrophils, airway epithelial cells) and cellular components.

**Classification**:
- Allergic asthma
- Non-allergic asthma
- Late-onset asthma
- Severe asthma

### 1.3 Pneumonia

#### 1.3.1 Classification
- By anatomy: Lobar pneumonia, lobular pneumonia, interstitial pneumonia
- By etiology: Bacterial pneumonia, viral pneumonia, mycoplasma pneumonia, fungal pneumonia
- By setting of acquisition: Community-acquired pneumonia, hospital-acquired pneumonia

## Chapter 2: Cardiovascular Diseases

### 2.1 Hypertension

#### 2.1.1 Definition and Classification
Hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure ≥140 mmHg and/or diastolic blood pressure ≥90 mmHg on at least 3 separate occasions without antihypertensive medication.

**Blood Pressure Classification**:
- Normal blood pressure: SBP <120 mmHg and DBP <80 mmHg
- Normal high: SBP 120-139 mmHg and/or DBP 80-89 mmHg
- Hypertension:
  - Grade 1: SBP 140-159 mmHg and/or DBP 90-99 mmHg
  - Grade 2: SBP ≥160 mmHg and/or DBP ≥100 mmHg

### 2.2 Coronary Heart Disease

#### 2.2.1 Pathophysiology
Coronary atherosclerosis is the main cause of coronary heart disease. Its pathological changes include lipid deposition, fibrous tissue proliferation, plaque formation, ultimately leading to coronary artery lumen stenosis or obstruction, causing myocardial ischemia, hypoxia, or necrosis.

**Clinical Types**:
- Asymptomatic myocardial ischemia
- Angina pectoris
- Myocardial infarction
- Ischemic cardiomyopathy
- Sudden cardiac death

## Chapter 3: Digestive System Diseases

### 3.1 Peptic Ulcer Disease

#### 3.1.1 Etiology and Pathogenesis
Peptic ulcer disease mainly refers to chronic ulcers occurring in the stomach and duodenum, namely gastric ulcer (GU) and duodenal ulcer (DU).

**Main Causes**:
- Helicobacter pylori (Hp) infection
- Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAIDs) use
- Gastric acid and pepsin
- Genetic factors
- Other factors: Smoking, alcohol, stress, etc.

**Pathogenesis**:
Imbalance between defensive factors (mucosal barrier, bicarbonate secretion, blood flow) and aggressive factors (acid, pepsin, Hp, NSAIDs) in the stomach and duodenum leads to peptic ulcer development.
