# Type 2 Diabetes Prevention and Treatment Guidelines (2020 Edition)

## 1. Overview

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) is a common chronic metabolic disease characterized by insulin resistance and insufficient insulin secretion, leading to elevated blood glucose levels. This guideline aims to provide clinicians with standardized diagnosis and treatment recommendations to improve the prevention and treatment of type 2 diabetes.

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria

### 2.1 Diabetes Diagnostic Criteria
- Glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c) ≥6.5%
- Fasting plasma glucose (FPG) ≥7.0 mmol/L
- 2-hour plasma glucose during oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) ≥11.1 mmol/L
- Random plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L with classic hyperglycemia symptoms

### 2.2 Prediabetes Diagnostic Criteria
- Impaired fasting glucose (IFG): 6.1≤FPG<7.0 mmol/L
- Impaired glucose tolerance (IGT): 7.8≤OGTT 2-hour glucose<11.1 mmol/L
- Glycated hemoglobin: 5.7%≤HbA1c<6.5%

## 3. Treatment Pathway

### 3.1 Lifestyle Intervention
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

All type 2 diabetes patients should receive lifestyle intervention, including:
- Rational diet: Control total calories, properly distribute carbohydrates, protein, and fat
- Regular exercise: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week
- Weight control: Keep BMI below 24 kg/m²
- Quit smoking and limit alcohol
- Psychological balance

### 3.2 Glycemic Control Targets
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

- General adults: HbA1c <7.0%
- Elderly patients (over 65): HbA1c <7.5% (good health) or <8.0% (poor health)
- Gestational diabetes: HbA1c <6.5%
- Patients with severe complications or high hypoglycemia risk: HbA1c <8.0%

### 3.3 Medication Treatment Pathway

#### 3.3.1 Monotherapy
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

- First-line: Metformin: Starting dose 500mg, 2-3 times daily, maximum dose 2000mg/day
- If metformin is intolerant, alternatives include:
  - Sulfonylureas: Glimepiride 1-4mg/day
  - Thiazolidinediones: Pioglitazone 15-45mg/day
  - DPP-4 inhibitors: Sitagliptin 100mg/day
  - SGLT-2 inhibitors: Dapagliflozin 10mg/day

#### 3.3.2 Dual Therapy
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

If HbA1c target not achieved after 3 months of monotherapy, initiate dual therapy:
- Metformin + Sulfonylureas
- Metformin + Thiazolidinediones
- Metformin + DPP-4 inhibitors
- Metformin + SGLT-2 inhibitors
- Metformin + GLP-1 receptor agonists

#### 3.3.3 Triple Therapy
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1b

If HbA1c target not achieved after 3 months of dual therapy, initiate triple therapy:
- Metformin + Sulfonylureas + Thiazolidinediones
- Metformin + Sulfonylureas + DPP-4 inhibitors
- Metformin + Sulfonylureas + SGLT-2 inhibitors
- Metformin + Sulfonylureas + GLP-1 receptor agonists

#### 3.3.4 Insulin Therapy
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

If HbA1c target not achieved after 3 months of triple therapy, initiate insulin therapy:
- Basal insulin: Insulin glargine, starting dose 0.1-0.2 U/kg/day
- Premixed insulin: Insulin aspart 30, starting dose 0.2-0.4 U/kg/day, injected before breakfast and dinner
- Basal-bolus insulin: Insulin aspart + insulin glargine, adjust dose according to blood glucose

## 4. Complication Screening and Management

### 4.1 Macrovascular Complications
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

- Cardiovascular disease: Annual ECG examination, assess cardiovascular risk
- Cerebrovascular disease: Regular carotid ultrasound examination
- Lower extremity vascular disease: Annual ankle-brachial index (ABI) examination

### 4.2 Microvascular Complications
**Recommendation Grade**: A
**Evidence Level**: 1a

- Diabetic kidney disease: Annual urine albumin/creatinine ratio (UACR) and serum creatinine examination
- Diabetic retinopathy: Annual fundus examination
