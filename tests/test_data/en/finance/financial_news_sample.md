# Weekly Macro Report: PBoC Cuts RRR by 50bps, Releasing Approximately RMB 1 Trillion in Liquidity

## January 13, 2025

**Author**: James Chen, Senior Macro Analyst, Beijing

---

## Key Events

### PBoC RRR Cut

The People's Bank of China announced on Friday that it will cut the reserve requirement ratio for financial institutions by 0.5 percentage points on January 15 (excluding financial institutions already subject to 5% RRR). After the cut, the weighted average RRR for financial institutions will be approximately 6.6%.

This is the first RRR cut by the PBoC since September 2024, and is expected to release approximately RMB 1 trillion in long-term liquidity.

The PBoC stated in its announcement that this RRR cut aims to "maintain reasonable and sufficient liquidity, promote a steady decline in comprehensive financing costs, and support the development of the real economy."

### Market Reactions

After the RRR cut announcement, financial markets responded positively:

- **A-Share Market**: The Shanghai Composite Index rose 0.62% to close at 3,256.39 points; the Shenzhen Component Index rose 0.83% to close at 10,978.35 points
- **Bond Market**: The 10-year Treasury yield dropped 4 basis points to 2.45%
- **RMB Exchange Rate**: The onshore RMB against USD rate remained basically flat at 7.24
- **Bank Stocks**: China Merchants Bank rose 3.2%, Bank of China rose 2.8%

---

## Policy Interpretation

### Background of RRR Cut

**1. Need for Economic Stabilization and Growth**

December 2024 CPI rose 0.1% year-over-year, PPI fell 2.7% year-over-year, with low inflation levels. GDP grew 4.8% for the full year 2024, below the 5% target set at the beginning of the year. The economy faces downward pressure and requires monetary policy support.

**2. Maintaining Reasonable Liquidity Abundance**

January is traditionally a peak season for credit issuance, plus increased cash demand before the Spring Festival, the banking system faces greater liquidity pressure. The RRR cut can inject liquidity into the banking system in advance to meet credit demand.

**3. Coordinating with Fiscal Policy**

The fiscal deficit ratio for 2025 is expected to increase to 4%, requiring monetary policy coordination. The RRR cut can reduce banks' liability costs and create conditions for banks to support the real economy.

### Impact of RRR Cut

**Impact on Banks**:
- Expected to reduce banks' liability costs by approximately RMB 150 billion
- Improve bank net interest margin by approximately 1-2 basis points
- Increase banks' loanable funds

**Impact on Real Economy**:
- Loan Prime Rate (LPR) is expected to further decline
- Corporate financing costs decline
- Real estate financing environment marginally improves

**Impact on Markets**:
- Short-term positive for stock market
- Bond market yield decline space opens up
- RMB exchange rate remains relatively stable

## Institutional Views

### Brokerage Views

**CICC**: The PBoC RRR cut slightly exceeded expectations; LPR is expected to decline 5-10 basis points in Q1. Maintaining the forecast of 2 RRR cuts and 1 interest rate cut in 2025.

**Huatai Securities**: The RRR cut directly benefits bank stocks; continuing to favor high-quality bank stocks such as China Merchants Bank and Ningbo Bank.

**Guotai Junan**: The RRR cut benefits A-shares, especially financial and cyclical stocks. However, the economic fundamentals still need more policy support.

### Institution Views

**Goldman Sachs**: Expects China's 2025 GDP growth at 4.5%, with increased policy easing intensity.

**Morgan Stanley**: The RRR cut is a positive policy signal, but insufficient to completely offset downward pressure from the real estate sector.

## Policy Outlook

### Monetary Policy

**Short-term**:
- LPR expected to decline 5-10 basis points in Q1
- Structural monetary policy tools continue to play a role
- Refinancing scale may expand

**Full Year Outlook**:
- RRR cut 1-2 times (50 basis points each time)
- MLF rate decline once (approximately 10 basis points)
- LPR decline 1-2 times (total 10-20 basis points)

### Fiscal Policy

- Fiscal deficit ratio for 2025 expected to increase to 4%
- New special bond quota approximately RMB 4.5 trillion
- Ultra-long-term special Treasury bond issuance approximately RMB 1 trillion
- Acceleration of local government implicit debt replacement

## Risk Alerts

1. **Real Estate Risk**: Continuous decline in real estate sales and investment may trigger systemic risk
2. **Local Debt Risk**: Local government debt pressure is relatively large, requiring proper policy resolution
3. **External Uncertainty**: Federal Reserve monetary policy direction and geopolitical changes may affect domestic markets
4. **Inflation Risk**: If pork prices rise sharply, it may push up CPI
