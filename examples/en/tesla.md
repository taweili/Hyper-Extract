# Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

## Early Life and Education

Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in the village of Smiljan, which was then part of the Austrian Empire (now modern-day Croatia). His father was an Orthodox priest, and his mother was an inventor of household appliances.

Tesla studied engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz. He was an exceptional student, particularly in mathematics and physics. He later attended the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague, but left after one semester due to the death of his father.

## Working with Edison

In 1882, Tesla moved to Budapest and worked at the Central Telephone Exchange. There, he conceived the idea for the induction motor, a device that uses alternating current to create a rotating magnetic field.

In 1884, Tesla emigrated to the United States and arrived in New York City with little more than the clothes on his back and four cents in his pocket. He went to work for Thomas Edison, the famous inventor who was then establishing the Edison Electric Light Company.

Tesla worked for Edison for about a year. According to Tesla's own account, he was offered $50,000 to redesign Edison's inefficient motors and generators. He successfully completed the task but was never paid the reward. This led to the famous "War of Currents" between Tesla and Edison.

## War of Currents

Tesla left Edison and founded the Tesla Electric Company in 1887. He developed the AC induction motor and the Tesla coil, which were essential components for the AC power system.

George Westinghouse, founder of Westinghouse Electric Company, bought the patents to Tesla's AC system. This sparked the "War of Currents" between Edison (who advocated DC power) and Tesla/Westinghouse (who advocated AC power).

Edison conducted a public relations campaign to discredit AC power, even demonstrating the dangers of AC by electrocuting animals. Tesla famously demonstrated the safety of AC by allowing himself to be subjected to AC current during public presentations.

In 1893, the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago chose AC power for its electrical system, marking a major victory for Tesla. The contract to generate electricity for the exposition was won by Westinghouse.

## Colorado Springs

In 1899, Tesla moved to Colorado Springs to conduct experiments on wireless power transmission. He built a laboratory there and created the largest Tesla coil ever constructed, standing over 50 feet tall.

During this period, Tesla transmitted radio signals across long distances and received what he believed were signals from extraterrestrial intelligence. He called them "interplanetary messages."

## Wardenclyffe Tower

In 1901, Tesla began construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower in Shoreham, New York. This was an ambitious project intended to transmit messages and power wirelessly across the Atlantic Ocean.

However, financial troubles plagued the project. His main backer, J.P. Morgan, withdrew support when Tesla could not provide a working prototype for commercial wireless telegraphy.

The project was never completed, and the tower was demolished in 1917. This marked the beginning of Tesla's decline in fortunes.

## Later Years and Death

Tesla spent his later years living in hotels in New York City. He became increasingly eccentric and solitary. Despite his financial troubles, he continued to work on inventions.

In his 78th year, Tesla claimed he could split the Earth like an apple. He wrote a manifesto about what he called "telautodynamics," describing a system for transmitting mechanical power using radio waves.

Tesla died alone in Room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel on January 7, 1943. He was 86 years old and reportedly worth just $2.30 at the time of his death.

## Notable Relationships

### Rivals
- Thomas Edison: His former employer and main rival in the "War of Currents"
- Edison's DC supporters: They conducted a campaign to discredit Tesla's AC system
- Guglielmo Marconi: Italian inventor who competed with Tesla over radio patents

### Partners and Supporters
- George Westinghouse: His key business partner who commercialized his AC patents
- J.P. Morgan: Initial financial backer of Wardenclyffe Tower project

### Intellectuals
- Mark Twain: Famous American writer who was a close friend and visited Tesla's laboratory
- Swami Vivekananda: Indian philosopher who discussed science and religion with Tesla
